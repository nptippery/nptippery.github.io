Menyanthaceae Photographs

    Below are photographs of Menyanthaceae species taken by Nicholas Tippery. The images are available for public or private non-commercial use with acknowledgement of authorship. Please contact the webmaster with questions or comments, or to report use of the images.

Nymphoides aurantiaca (Dalzell) Kuntze

These plants were photographed in the Northern Territory of Australia.


Growth habit and long-styled flower.


Short-stlyed flower.

Nymphoides beaglensis Aston

These plants were photographed in the Kimberley region of Western Australia.


Short-stlyed flowers.


Long-stlyed flower.

Nymphoides crenata (F.Muell.) Kuntze

The photograph below was taken in Queensland, Australia. Nymphoides crenata is readily identified by its crenate (wavy) leaf margins and yellow or orange flowers with fringed lateral wings. The median wing is particularly diagnostic.


Short-stlyed flower.

Nymphoides cristata (Roxb.) Kuntze

The plant below was photographed in the University of Connecticut greenhouses. Although the species is native to India, the greenhouse plant originated from an adventive population of N. cristata in Florida, USA.


Nymphoides exiliflora (F.Muell.) Kuntze

These plants were photographed in Queensland, Australia.


Short-stlyed flower.


Long-stlyed flower.

Nymphoides furculifolia Specht

These plants were photographed in the Northern Territory of Australia. The epithet 'furculifolia' refers to the wishbone shape of the leaves.

The plants below were photographed in the Kimberley region of Western Australia.


Short-stlyed flower.

Limnanthemum thunbergianum var kalachariense Schinz
- Brown, N.E. in Thiselton-Dyer, W.T. ed. 1904. Flora of tropical Africa 4(1):584-585.
Calycis segmentis oblongo- lanceolatis, obtusis, quam capsula duplo brevioribus; capsula polysperma, globoso- ovoidea; seminibus carinatis, laevibus, ochraceis. Calyx segments oblong-lanceolate, obtuse, twice shorter than the capsule. Capsule many-seeded, globose-ovoid. Seeds keeled, smooth, ochre.
Südwest-Afrika (Kalachari): Bitterpits, Schinz 492, fr. V.
Laubblätter lederig, bis 18 cm lang und bis 14 cm breit. Blütenstände ± 25 blütig. Frucht kugelig eiörmig, bis doppelt so lang als die lanzettlichen stumpfen Kelchabschnitte, ± 70 Samen enthaltend. Samen lehmgelb, gekielt, glatt, glänzend. Leaves leathery, to 18 cm long and 14 cm wide. Inflorescence ± 25-flowered. Fruit globular-ovate, to twice as long as the obtuse-lanceolate calyx segments, containing ± 70 seeds. Seeds clay-yellow, keeled, smooth, shiny.
Die Früchte des L. Thunbergianum sind so lang oder kürzer als die Kelchzipfel und nur 6- bis 18samig; die Samen sind grau, dunkel gefleckt. Nun hängt aber sicherlich die Farbe der Samen vom Reifezustand derselben ab, denn wir besitzen unter unserem Limnanthemum-Material unzweifelhafte L. Thunbergianum mit z. T. grauen, z. T. lehmgelben Samen aus ein und derselben Kapsel. Vielleicht repräsentiert die Pflanze von Bitterpits eine von L. Thunbergianum verschiedene Art, ohne Blüten wage ich indessen keine Entscheidung. The fruits of L. thunbergianum are as long or shorter than the calyx tip and only 6- to 18-seeded; the seeds are gray, dark-mottled. Now, however, certainly the color of the seeds depends on their ripeness, because in our Limnanthemum material we have undoubted L. thunbergianum with sometimes gray, sometimes clay-yellow seeds from the same capsule. Perhaps the plant from Bitterpits represents a different type of L. thunbergianum; without flowers, however, I dare not decide.
Limnanthemum abyssinicum N.E.Br.
- Brown, N.E. in Thiselton-Dyer, W.T. ed. 1904. Flora of tropical Africa 4(1):584-585.
Leaves ½-4 in. [1-10 cm] in diam., orbicular, with a deep acute sinus at the base, very thin and membranous. False petioles ¾-1⅓ lin. [1.5-2.8 mm] thick, bearing 10-12 or more flowers in a cluster, close to the leaf-blade. Pedicels ½-1 in. [1.2-2.5 cm] long, ⅓-½ lin. thick. Sepals 1½-2 lin. [3-4 mm] long, oblong-lanceolate, subacute or obtuse. Corolla not seen. Fruit globose, exceeding the sepals, 10-20-seeded. Seeds ¾ lin. [1.6 mm] in diam., ½ lin. [1.0 mm] thick, compressed-globose, subcarinate, apiculate at the basal end, smooth, opaque, light ochreous.
Nile Land. Abyssinia: without precise locality, Schimper, 1358!
Limnanthemum brevipedicellatum Vatke
- Vatke, G.C.W. 1876. Labiatas a d. Dr. W. Peters in itinere mossambiciensi collectas in opere klotzschiano omissas enumerat. Linnaea: ein Journal für die Botanik in ihrem ganzen Umfange 40:220.
Foliis late cordato-orbiculatis utrinque secus medianam asperiusculis sparse pedicellisque glanduloso-punctatis obsolete palmatinerviis, nervis subtus prominentibus, calycis segmentis ovato-lanceolatis capsulam subaequantibus, corolla ....., [sic] seminibus compresso-globosis obtuse carinatis opacis minutissime punctato-foveolatis. Leaves broadly cordate-orbiculate, each [secus medianam], sparsely roughened, and with glandulose-punctate pedicels, barely palmately veined, the veins prominent beneath. Calyx segments ovate-lanceolate, less than equal to the capsule in length. Corolla ... [sic]. Seeds compressed-globose, obtusely keeled, opaque, minutely punctate-foveolate.
A L. forbesiano Griseb. foliis multo majoribus, pedicellis brevioribus, seminibus compressis differt, a L. orbiculato Griseb. mihi ignoto seminibus carinatis non vero ut in L. nilotico Kotschy et Peyritsch pl. Tinn. 28 t. IX! muricatis; folia adulta ultra 1 dm. longa, ad 1 dm. lata; pedicelli fructiferi 1 cm. longi fere omnes fertiles subaequilongi. Differs from L. forbesianum Griseb. in having much larger leaves, shorter pedicels, compressed seeds. Differs from L. orbiculatum Griseb. (a species unknown to me) in having keeled seeds, truly not muricate as in L. niloticum Kotsch. & Peyr. (Pl. Tinn. 28 t. IX!). Adult leaves up to 1 dm long, to 1 dm broad. Fruit-bearing pedicels 1 cm long, all fertile leaves about equal.
Typus: In lacu Zana prope Galamotseh 9 nov. 1863 fr. (n. 1358.) Type: In Lake Zana near Galamotseh. 9 Nov 1863 fr. (n. 1358).

Nymphoides furculifolia Specht
- Specht, R.L., Mountford, C.P. 1958. Records of the American-Australian scientific expedition to Arnhem Land. vol. 3: botany and plant ecology. p. 280.
Herba aquatica similis Nymphoidi minimae sed folia aqua sustenta, obtusa, sagittiformes, auriculis longis et obtusis ad angulam 45° ad laminam. Lamina 3-4 mm. longa, 2 mm. lata, auriculis 8-10 mm. longis et 1.5-2.0 mm. latis. Aquatic herb, similar to Nymphoides minima, but leaves supported by the water, obtuse, arrowhead-shaped, with long and obtuse auricles at a 45° angle. Lamina 3-4 mm long, 2 mm broad, with auricles 8-10 mm long and 1.5-2.0 mm broad.
Habit Aquatic herb. Filiform stems 20-40 cm. long from a submerged tuft of oblanceolate leaves 2-3 cm. long, 1-2 mm. wide, resembling a petiole and bearing a single terminal floating leaf, nearly sessile, and just below the true, exceedingly short petiole an unilateral tuft of pedicels. Floating leaves, obtuse sagittiform with long, rounded auricles directed outwards at an angle of 45° to the leaf blade. Leaf blade 3-4 mm. long, 2 mm. wide. Auricles 8-10 mm. long, 1.5-2.0 mm. wide. Flowers Calyx segments scarcely 2 mm. long. Corolla white 2-3 mm. long. Fruit Capsule about as long as the calyx, with a few small, smooth seeds.
This species is closely allied to Nymphoides minima (F.Muell.) Kuntze from which it differs in the sagittiform leaves.
South Bay, Bickerton Island (waterhole in the sandstone hills): 455. Hyd. Type - Brisbane (BRI).

Villarsia calthifolia F.Muell.
- von Mueller, F. 1868. Fragmenta Phytographiae Australiae. v. 6. p. 136-142.
Erecta, robusta, glabra, foliis magnis v. maximis carnulentis orbiculato-cordatis calloso-dentatis, petiolis crassis radicalibus longissimis, caulinis folio fere aequilongis, cymis compositis corymboso-paniculatis, sepalis late ovatis acutis, corollae luteae lobis ecristatis margine nudis, fauce fimbriata, capsula ovato- v. globoso-urceolata calycem paulo superante polysperma infra apicem truncatum circumscissum breviter valvata fere membranea, placentis ad dodrantem per cavitatem extensis, seminibus livido-fulvidis majusculis turgide ovatis immarginatis dense asperulatis ad umbilicum circularem supra-basalem paulo turgidulis. Erect, robust, glabrous. Leaves large or very large, fleshy, orbiculate-cordate, callous-dentate. Radical petioles thick and very long, cauline petioles about equal in lengh to the laminae. Cyme composite, corymbose-paniculate. Sepals acute, broadly ovate. Lobes of the yellow corolla without a median wing, bare at the margin, with fimbriate throat. Capsule ovate- or globose-urceolate, exceeding the calyx a little, many-seeded, beneath the truncate apex circumscissile, briefly valvate, almost membranaceous. Placentae extended to three-fourths through the cavity. Seeds leaden-yellow, somewhat larger swollen ovate, immarginate, densely roughened, a little turgid at the circular, supra-basal hilum.
In rivulis et in summitatibus rupestribus montium Porongorup. In streams and on the rocky summits of the Porungorup mountains.
Petioli radicales bipedales v. breviores. Folia iis formarum majorum Calthae palustris non absimilia, radicalia plura v. numerosa, interdum fere pedem longa et lata, margine irregulariter seu erose dentata, subtus glandulis callosis conspersa; sinus angustus v. clausus; folia caulina saepe 2-3-uncialia; nervatio omnium haud valde prominens; ramuli venularum in areolis venarum liberi. Bracteae inferiores foliaceae, rhombeo- v. lanceolato-ovatae, 1-1½" longae. Panicula confertiflora, caulem v. ramum terminans. Pedunculus primarius pluriuncialis v. brevior. Pedicelli calyce duplo breviores vel usque ad duplum longiores. Bracteolae latius angustiusve lanceolatae, paucilineares. Calyces circa 4"' longi. Corolla fere ea V. parnassifoliae et V. reniformis. Antherae oblongo-ovatae, apiculatae. Glandulae basales 5 renatae. Stylus circiter semilinearis. Capsula ad collum constricta, inferne turgens; operculum fere 1"' latum; orificium passim 8-dentatum. Semina circiter ⅔"' longa. Radical petioles 2 ft [60cm] or shorter. Leaves not unlike those of the larger form of Caltha palustris. Radical leaves many or numerous, occasionally about 1 ft [30 cm] long and broad, the margin irregularly erose or dentate. Leaf underside callous, scattered with glands. Sinus narrow or closed. Cauline leaves often 2-3 in [5-8 cm]. Venation on the whole scarcely prominent, the branchings of venules free in areas of veins. Lower bracts leafy, rhomboid- or lanceolate-ovate, 1-1.5 in [2-4 cm] long. Panicle with flowers borne together, terminating the stem or a branch. Primary peduncle several inches [1 in equals 2.5 cm] or shorter. Pedicels shorter than the calyx by half, or often up to two times longer. Bracteoles broadly and narrowly lanceolate, a few lines [1 lin equals 2 mm] long. Calyces about 4 lin [8mm] long. Corolla approximate to that of V. parnassifolia and V. reniformis. Anthers oblong-ovate, apiculate. Glands five, reniform, basal. Style around 0.5 lin [1 mm]. Capsule constricted at the neck, swelling beneath, the opening about 1 lin [2 mm] across, the orifice randomly eight-toothed. Seeds around 0.7 lin [1.5 mm] long.
Ad hancce speciem traho plantam Drummondi 5. To this species I assign the plant Drummond 5.
Villarsia latifolia gaudet capsula globoso-ovata, calyce superata, chartaceo-crustacea, oligosperma, distincte valvata; seminibus fulvis, laevibus, nitentibus, fere 1"' longis, turgide ovatis, strophiolam ferme semiglobosam laterali-basalem conspicuam producentibus, immarginatis. Caulis magis foliatus et folia magis membranea, quam in V. reniformi. Cl. P. Walcott plantam a fluvio Blackwood's River misit. Corolla expansa calycem saltem breviter superans. Radix fibrosa, forsan annua. Huc pertinent plantae Drummondi 3 et fortasse 4. Villarsia latifolia enjoys a globose-ovate capsule, surpassed by the calyx, papery-crustaceous, few-seeded, distinctly valvate. Seeds yellow, smooth, shining, about 1lin [2mm] long, plumply ovate, immarginate, producing a conspicuous, almost semiglobose, lateral-basal strophiole. Stem more foliate and leaves more membranaceous than in V. reniformis. Cl. P. Walcott sent a plant from Blackwood's River, the expanded corolla shortly surpassing the calyx by a little. Root fibrous, perhaps annual. Hence pertain Drummond 3 and possibly 4.
Indagatio omnium specierum denuo in libera natura necesse erit. Configuratio enim alarum corollae et aliae notae e corollis emarcidis aegre recognoscuntur. Caulis anatomia, sicut venarum per folia cursus tantum in plantae statu vivo bene intelliguntur. A renewed investigation of all species must be made in free nature. The configuration of the wings of the corolla and other aspects noted from corollas that weakly wither away are recognized. Anatomy of the stem, like the course of veins through leaves, is so well known in the living state of the plant.

Villarsia reniformis R.Br.
- von Mueller, F. 1868. Fragmenta Phytographiae Australiae. v. 6p. 136-142.
Perennis, erecta, glabra, foliis ovatis v. cordatis v. ovato- v. renato-cordatis radicalibus longe v. longissime et valide petiolatis crassiusculis integerrimis v. repandis, caule aphyllo v. monophyllo, panicula terminali, calycis segmentis acutatis, corollae luteae lobis ecristatis margine nudis, fauce denso-fimbriata, stylo brevi, stigmatibus deltoideo-rotundatis, capsula fere ovata pleio- v. oligosperma calyce superata vel ei aequilonga valvata crustacea, placentis alte extensis, seminibus magnis lenticulari-ovatis laevibus strophila conspicua circulari basali praeditus fulvo-lividis immarginatis. Perennial, erect,, glabrous. Leaves ovate or cordate or ovate- or reniform-cordate, radical, longly or very longly and strongly petiolate, a little thick, entire or repand. Stem leafless or one-leaved. Panicle terminal. Calyx segments acute. Lobes of the yellow corolla without a median wing, bare at the margin. Corolla throat dense-fimbriate. Style short. Stigmas deltoid-rotund. Capsule quite ovate, many- or few-seeded, surpassed by the calyx or of equal length to it, with crustaceous valves. Placentae extended deeply. Seeds large, lenticular-ovate, smooth, with a conspicuous circular basal strophiole, yellow-leaden, immarginate.
In toto tractu inter sinus Spenceri et Moretoni jacente, alpes etiam adscendens. In the entire tract between Spencer Bay and Moreton, even ascending the mountains.
Planta spithamaea usque pluripedalis, in limosis passim procumbens nec repens. Petioli sesquipedales v. breviores. Folia 3½" longa vel breviora, acutiuscula v. obtusissima, haud eminenter nervosa. Bractea ad basim rami infimi paniculae foliiformis, anguste lanceolata, petiolata. Calyces semipollicares v. breviores. Corollae lobi, ut in plerisque Goodeniaceis, truncato-alati. Capsula 3-4"' longa; valvae bifidae, apice recurvae. Placentae e divisura utriusque valvae descendentes, ante valvae basim evanescentes. Semina iis quarumdam Goodeniarum similia, ¾"' rarius ½"' metientia, fere rotunda, subtilissime punctulato-impressa, rarissime papilloso-aspera. Upright plant to several feet [1 ft equals 30 cm] tall. In mud possibly procumbent, but not creeping. Petioles 1.5ft [45 cm] or shorter. Leaves 3.5in [8.9 cm] long or shorter, rather acute or very obtuse, veins hardly prominent. Bract at the base of the lowest branch of the panicle foliose, narrow lanceolate, petiolate. Calyces 1.5in [3.8 cm] or shorter. Corolla lobes, as in most Goodeniaceae, truncate-winged. Capsule 3-4 lin [6.4-8.5 mm] long; valves bifid, recurved at the apex. Placentae descending from the division of each valve, before the valves disappear at the base. Seeds similar to those of certain Goodenia, measuring 0.8 lin [1.7 mm], rarely 0.5 lin [1.1 mm], quite rotund, very subtly punctate-impressed, very rarely papillose-roughened.
Planta non solum habitu Velleyis similis, sed etiam corollae aestivatione induplicato-valvata et pluribus aliis notis. A plant similar not only in habit to Velleya (Goodeniaceae), but also in induplicate-valvate corolla aestivation and many other features of note.
Folia nunquam rite renata, quam ob rem et propter nomenclaturae confusionem haecce species nunc melius V. exaltata appelletur. Leaves never truly reniform. For this reason and because of the confusion of nomenclature, this species now should better be named V. exaltata.
A fluvio Wannon habeo plantam seminibus minoribus ovatis turgentibus estrophiolatis asperis saltem a forma legitima diversam. I have a plant from the Wannon River which differs from the legitimate form at least in having smaller, ovate, swollen, roughened seeds without a strophiole.

- Grisebach A. 1839. Genera et Species Gentianearum. p. 336-348.
Foliis reniformibus basi cordatis integerrimis, scapo elongato. Leaves reniform, cordate at the base, quite entire. Scape elongate.
Menyanthes sarmentosa Sims
- Sims, J. 1810. Botanical Magazine. v. 32. p. 1328.
(Natans) flagellis reptantibus, foliis cordato-suborbicularis repandis subtus punctatis, paniculis, oppositifoliis, seminibus glabris. (Floating) with creeping stolons. Leaves cordate-suborbicular, repand, punctate beneath. Panicle opposite-leaved. Seeds glabrous.
This species belongs to the genus Villarsia of Ventenat, adopted by Brown. We have before given our reasons in part for not following this change; to which we may now add, that Brown himself seems to give up the character drawn from the slight difference in the attachemnt of the seeds, observing that Menyanthes chiefly differs from Villarsia by the disk of the corolla being bearded lengthwise with naked margins, and by its ternate leaves.
We cannot ascertain that this species is mentioned by Mr. Brown in his Flora of New-Holland; but as these plants seem to be extremely variable, it may possibly belong to his Villarsia geminata; for the terminal pedicles in this are generally in pairs. But as he makes no mention of the remarkable runners, several feet in length, putting out leaves and roots, at intervals of about a foot and a half, in the same manner as the strawberry plant, we think it safest to consider it as a different species, especially as he himself doubts whether his two varieties may not be distinct; and if so, the name of geminata may remain to his second variety.
We were first favoured with this plant, by Mr. Kent of Clapton; but the flowers of his plant never properly expanding with us, our drawing was taken at Mr. Knight's, King's-Road, Little Chelsea; where, from growing in a large stone cistern, the plant had room to shew its natural habit, and flowered very strong.
Native of New-Holland. Flowers in June. Requires the protection of a greenhouse to defend it from frost.
Villarsia reniformis R.Br.
- Brown, R. 1810. Prodromus Florae Novae Hollandiae. p. 457.
Foliis radicalibus reniformibus integris, caule elongato nudiusculo, floribus paniculatis. Radical leaves reniform, entire. Stem elongate, rather bare. Flowers paniculate.

Villarsia chilensis Lodd.
- Loddiges, C. 1833. Botanical Cabinet 20: 1994.
This is a native of Chili [Chile], and has been very lately introduced. It flowers in June with a stem about a foot in height: the flowers are very pretty, and open a few at a time, in succession, each lasting but a short while.
It will increase by separating the root, and should be potted in rich loam, with a pretty large allowance of water.
Villarsia parnassifolia (Labill.) R.Br.
- Brown, R. 1810. Prodromus Florae Novae Hollandiae. p. 457.
Foliis radicalibus ovato-cordatis repando-dentatis, caule elongato nudiusculo, floribus paniculatis, corollae laciniarum marginibus integris, capsula calycem superante: valvis bifidis, seminibus scabriusculus. Radical leaves ovate-cordate, repand-dentate. Stem elongate, rather bare. Flowers paniculate. Margins of corolla lobes entire. Capsule surpassing the calyx: valves bifid. Seeds rather roughened.
Swertia parnassifolia Labill.
- La Billardière, J. J. H. 1806. Novae Hollandiae plantarum specimen. v. 1. p. 72. t. 97.
Corollis quinquepartitis; foliis radicalibus cordatis; caulinis lanceolatis, alternis. Corollas five-parted. Radical leaves cordate, cauline leaves lanceolate, alternate.
Caulis sesquipedalis, dichotomus, subcompressus. Folia radicalia numerosa, cordata, subrepanda, quinque ad septemnervia, petiolis longissimis, compressis, infra dilatatis. Folia vero caulina perpauca, lanceolata, brevia, superiora scilicet bracteiformia, inferiora nonnunquam radicalibus conformia, omnia alterna. Bractea lanceolata, pedicellis multo brevior, sub singulis paniculae dichotomae divisionibus apposita. Calyx inflatus, turbinatus, quinquepartitus, laciniis ovalibus, oblongis. Corolla tubo brevissimo, quinquepartita, laciniis lanceolatis, lutea, intus pilosa, marcescens. Staminum filamenta quinque brevia, corollae tubo inserta, laciniis alterna; antherae biloculares, lanceolatae, infra bifidae. Glandulae quinque suborbiculatae, minimae, insertae sub corolla ejusdemque laciniis oppositae. Germen ovatum, stylo breviori; stigmata duo acuta. Capsula supera, oblonga, unilocularis, bivalvis, receptaculo longitudinali in medio valvulae cujusvis supra bifidae. Semina plurima, globosa, tenuissime scrobiculata, pallida, straminea. Corculum teretiusculum in perispermo carnoso. Stems 1.5 ft [50 cm], dichotomous, subcompressed. Radical leaves numerous, cordate, subrepand, 5-7-veined; petioles very long, compressed, swollen at the base. Cauline leaves truly very few, lanceolate, short; those toward the top somewhat bract-like, the lower ones resemble the radical leaves; all leaves are alternate. Bracts lanceolate, much shorter than the pedicels, positioned beneath a single division of the dichotomous panicle. Calyx inflated, conical, five-parted, with oval, oblong lobes. Corolla with a very short tube, five-parted, with lanceolate lobes, yellow, hairy within, drooping. Stamen filaments five, short, inserted on the corolla tube, alternate to the petals; anthers bilocular, lanceolate, bifid within. Glands five, suborbiculate, minimal, inserted under the same corolla opposite to the petals. Ovary ovate, with a rather short style; stigmas two, acute. Capsule conspicuous, oblong, unilocular, bivalvate, valves bifid above longitudinally midway to the receptacle. Seeds many, globose, shallowly furrowed, pale, straw-colored. Embryo smooth in a fleshy perisperm.
Habitat in terra Van-Leuwin. Inhabits Van-Leeuwin land. [SW Australia]

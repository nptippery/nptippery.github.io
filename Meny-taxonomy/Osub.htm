Villarsia submersa Aston
- Aston, H.I. 1969. The genus Villarsia (Menyanthaceae) in Australia. Muelleria 2: 3-63.
Herba gracillima, omnino submersa (praeter flores), caespitosa, radicata, perennis. Foliorum radicalium laminae ellipticae usque ad ovato-lanceolatas, 2.3-6 cm. x 1-2.6 (-3), quam latae bis terque longiores, dorsiventrales, petiolis ad 40 cm. longis praeditae. Culmus usque ad 60 cm. longus. Inflorescentia laxe paniculata, multiramosa, ramulis vel pedicellis 1-5 ad notos simuli emergentibus; pedicelli 20-70 mm. longi. Flores parvi; calycis lobi 3.5-4.5 mm. longi; corolla alba, quam calyx circiter bis longior, intus lanata vel pilosa. Capsula 3-3.5 mm. longa, quam lobi calycis circiter 1 mm. brevior, tenue membranacea, ellipsoidea, irregulariter dehiscens ut videtur. Semen parvum, 0.6-0.75 (-0.8) mm. longum, glabrum, ellipsoideum, aliquanto lateraliter compressum, stramineum ad fulvum. Very slender herb, on the whole submersed (except the flowers), caespitose, rooted, perennial. Laminae of the radical leaves elliptic to ovate-lanceolate, 1.3-6 cm x 1-2.6 (-3), two or three times longer than broad, dorsiventral, petioles to 40 cm long. Culm up to 60 cm long. Inflorescence laxly paniculate, many-branched, branches or pedicels 1-5 emerging at the same nodes; pedicels 20-70 mm long. Flowers small; calyx lobes 3.5-4.5 mm long; corolla white, about twice longer than the calyx, wooly or pilose within. Capsule 3-3.5 mm long, around 1 mm shorter than the calyx lobes, thinly membranaceous, ellipsoid, irregularly dehiscent, as it seems. Seed small, 0.6-0.75 (-0.8) mm long, glabrous, ellipsoid, somewhat laterally compressed, straw-colored to yellow.
A speciebus omnibus aliis Villarsiae recedit ob habitum submersum gracillimumque et ob folia elliptica (quem lata multo longiora). Eius semina ad haec V. parnassifoliae (Labill.) R.Br. proxime accedunt. Differs from all other species of Villarsia in the submersed and slender habit, and in the elliptical leaves (which are much longer than broad). Its seeds approach closely to those of V. parnassifolia (Labill.) R.Br.

Villarsia albiflora F.Muell.
- von Mueller, F. 1860. Fragmenta Phytographiae Australiae. v. 2. p. 21-22.
Glabra, elatior, foliis radicalibus cordatis v. renatis repandis, caulinis ramos suffulcientibus lineari-lanceolatis bracteiformibus, panicula terminali longe ramosa laxa multiflora, calyce quinquepartito corollam semiaequante, corollae albae lobis anguste alatis acutis inferne fimbriato-barbatis margine nudis, stylo conspicuo, germine multigemmulato. Glabrous, rather elevated. Radical leaves cordate or reniform, repand. Cauline leaves bracteiform, linear-lanceolate, subtending branches. Panicle terminal, longly branched, lax, multi-flowered. Calyx five-parted, half the length of the corolla. Corolla lobes white, narrowly winged, acute, fimbriate-bearded within, bare at the margin. Style conspicuous. Ovary multiovulate.
In stagnis propre oppida Perth et Hampden. Clarke et Oldfield. In swamps near the town of Perth and Hampden. Clarke and Oldfield.
Herba saepe ulnaris, erecta, habitu Villarsiae parnassifoliae simillima. Folia radicalia longe petiolata, 1-2" lata. Corolla profunde 5-partita, simul cum barba alba. Filamenta antheris conspicue breviora, glandulis perigynis semiorbiculatis et corollae lobis alterna. Antherae flavae, lineari-oblongae, inferne sensim latiores, circiter 1⅓"' longae. Stylus fere 1"' metiens. Stigma ½"' longum, e lamellis 2 ovatis glabris constans. Germen conicum. Placentae alte extensae. Gemmulae late ovatae. Capsula adhuc ignota. Herb often an ulna [50 cm] in length, in habit most similar to V. parnassifolia. Radical leaves longly petiolate, 1-2 in [2-5 cm] broad. Corolla deeply 5-parted, together with white beard. Filaments conspicuously shorter than the anthers, alternate with the semiorbiculate perigynous glands and the corolla lobes. Anthers yellow, linear-oblong, gradually broader within, around 1.3 lin [2.8 mm] long. Style measuring about 1 lin [2 mm]. Stigma 0.5 lin [1 mm] long, consisting of two ovate, glabrous lamellae. Ovary conical. Placentae deeply extended. Ovules broadly ovate. Capsule thus far unknown.
Vera Vill. reniformis cum specie hinc exposita confundi nequit, propter corollam semper luteam lato- et truncato-alatam. Optimum forsan discremen, quo V. latifolia a speciebus affinibus distinguenda praebent semina, turgiditate et nigrescente colore eximia et nitore orbata, iis igitur Limnanthemi geminati similiora. V. latifoliam in vicinia fluminis Murchison reperit clar. Oldfield. The true V. reniformis cannot be confused with the species here exposed, on account of its always yellow, broad- and truncate-winged corolla. Perhaps seeds offer the best discerning feature, by which V. latifolia should be distinguished from allied species, excepted by their turgidity and darkness of color, and deprived of lustre; by these characters therefore it is more similar to Limnanthemum geminatum. Oldfield clearly finds V. latifoliam in the vicinity of the Murchison river.

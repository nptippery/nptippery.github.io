Nymphoides coronata (Dunn) Chun ex Y.D.Zhou & G.W.Hu
- Zhou, Y.D., Hu, G.W., Zhan, Q.H., and Q.F. Wang. 2014. Reappraisal of Nymphoides coronata (Menyanthaceae), a 100-year-lost species endemic to south China. Phytotaxa 184: 170-173.
Aquatic herbs, glabrous, submerged except leaves and flowers. Rhizome short, with numerous fibrous roots. Stems slender, carmine, with purple spots, branched, segmental, sometimes producing rootlets form the nodes. Leaves floating, leaf-blade ovate to orbicular, entire, upper surface green, lower surface purplish; petioles cylindric, basal extend into an amplexicaul sheath; basal leaves 2-3 cm in diameter, with 10-20 cm long petioles; cauline leaves smaller than basal ones, and with shorter petioles. Inflorescences cymose, axillary or terminal; bracts lanceolate, 5-7 × 3-5 mm, 2 opposite per node. Flowers always 2 per node, with 5-7 cm long pedicels, 5-merous, distylous. Calyx 5-lobed near base, lobes lanceolate, ca. 5-7 × 2-3 mm. Corolla golden yellow, 5-lobed near base, corolla tube 5-6 mm long; lobes with 3 fimbriate appendants at the base, obovate, 1.3-1.5 × 0.8-1.2 mm, margin broadly membranous and fimbriate, bifid at the tip. Stamens 5, insert on the corolla tube and alternate with lobes. Long styled flowers: filaments up to 1 mm long; ovary 1.5-2 mm wide; style 2-3 mm long; stigma 2-lobed, lobes lamelliform, fimbriate (Fig. 2BH). Short styled flowers: filaments 2-3 mm long; ovary 2-3 mm wide; style very short; stigma 2-lobed, lobes finger-like. Capsule indehiscent, obovate to obovate-elliptic, 8-9 × 4-5 mm, with persistent style 1-2 mm long. Seeds brownish black, ovoid to sub-globose, ca. 1 mm in diameter, 20-25 per capsule; seed coat densely spinescent.
Location and habitat: Longma Village, Wengtian Town, Wenchang, Hainan, China, 19°49'23"N, 110°52'39"E, elev. 30 m; in ponds or small ditches about 10 km from the shore. The water level less than 0.5 m, and the basal soil is sand.
Limnanthemum coronatum Dunn.
- Dunn, S.T. and W.J.Tutcher. 1912. Flora of Kwangtung and Hongkong (China). Bulletin of Miscellaneous Information, Additional Series X. Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew. London: Darling and Son, Ltd.
L. aurantiaco Dalz. affinis sed sepalis lineari-oblongis et coronae destributione [sic: correct to 'distributione'] distincta. Affined to L. aurantiacum Dalzell, but distinct in linear-oblong sepals and distribution of the corona.
Herba aquatica, glabra, praeter foliorum laminas floresque submersa. Folia fluitantia, reniformia, 1-2 cm. diam., integra, chartacea; petioli 3-7 cm. longi, basi stipulis adnatisa lata [sic: correct to 'adnatis alata']. Flores saepissime solitarii, axillares, ex aqua paullo [sic] protrusi. Sepala 5, oblongo-linearia, 6 mm. longa. Corolla flava, rotata, 2-2.5 cm. diam., 5-lobata; lobi oblongi, apice fimbriati, 1.2 cm. longi, tubo bis longiores, basi cristati, coronam pulchram fimbriatam 5 mm. longam supra os formantes. Stamina 5, in tubo medio inserta et eum paullo [sic] excedentia; antherae oblongae, sagittatae. Stigma in lamellas duas fimbriatas divisum. Aquatic, glabrous herb, submersed except for the laminae of leaves and flowers. Leaves floating, reniform, 1-2 cm in diameter, entire, membranaceous. Petioles 3-7 cm long, winged at the base, with adnate stipules. Flowers most often solitary, axillary, protruted a little from the water. Sepals five, oblong-linear, 6 mm long. Corolla yellow, rotate, 2.0-2.5 cm in diameter, five-lobed. Lobes oblong, fimbriate at the apex, 1.2 cm long, twice as long as the tube, crested at the base, forming a lovely, fimbriate, 5 mm long corona above the mouth. Stamens five, inserted on the tube middle and exceeding it a little. Anthers oblong, sagittate. Stigma divided into two fimbriate lobes.
Kwaisin, Hoifung, Native Collector, Hongk. Herb. n. 1651. Fl. yellow, September.

Villarsia Vent.
- Tippery, N.P. and D.H. Les. 2009. A new genus and new combinations in Australian Villarsia (Menyanthaceae). Novon 19: 406-413.
Aquatic or wetland perennial herbs. Radical leaves erect, with sheathing bases; petiolate with ovate or ellipsoid laminae, margins entire to crenate-dentate. Inflorescence paniculate; flowers 1 to 3 per node. Calyx persistent, divided into 5 lobes; corolla yellow, gamopetalous, 5-lobed, rotate, throat fimbriate, margins deeply fimbriate; stamens 5, alternate with corolla lobes, inserted on the corolla tube at the junction of the lobes; anthers 2-celled, sagittate, introrse; ovary unilocular with 2 parietal placentae; base surrounded by 5 glands; style solitary with 2 stigmas; ovules 1 to 4 per placenta. Capsule dehiscent into 4 valves, adnate to calyx only at the base; of equal length or shorter than the calyx; seeds 1.5–2.5 mm diam., orbicular, smooth or with bulliform protuberances, hilum terminal (angle between major axis and hilum with vertex at seed center < 30°), without a conspicuous caruncle.
Villarsia Vent.
- Ventenat, E.P. 1803. Choix de Plantes t. 9.
Calix 5-partitus, persistens. Corolla rotata, limbo saepius ciliato. Stylus brevissimus. Capsula 1-locularis, 2-valvis, valvularum marginibus incrassatis. Placenta in suturis capsulae. Semina duplici serie longitudinaliter digesta, margine membranaceo cincta. Calyx 5-parted, persistent. Corolla rotate, limb quite often ciliate. Style very short. Capsule unilocular, 2-valved, valve margins not thick. Placenta along the sutures of the capsule Seeds around twice as long as wide, bound by a membranaceous margin.

Villarsia sect. Foliosae Gilg
- Gilg, E. in Engler, A. and Prantl, K. 1895. II. 6. Menyanthoideae-Menyantheae (Gentianaceae). Die Natürlichen Pflanzenfamilien 4(2):105-108.
Blütentragende Stengel mit B. besetzt, welche von den Grundb. nicht verschieden sind. - Hierher V. calthifolia F.Muell., V. congestiflora F.Muell., V. capitata Nees, V. latifolia Benth., sämtlich auf Westaustralien beschränkt. Inflorescence axis with leaves that are not different from the basal leaves. - Includes V. calthifolia F.Muell., V. congestiflora F.Muell., V. capitata Nees, V. latifolia Benth., all limited to Western Australia.
Villarsia sect. Scaposae Gilg
- Gilg, E. in Engler, A. and Prantl, K. 1895. II. 6. Menyanthoideae-Menyantheae (Gentianaceae). Die Natürlichen Pflanzenfamilien 4(2):105-108.
Blütentragende Stengel schaftartig, blattlos oder mit 1 bis wenigen kleinen Blättchen besetzt, die von den Grundb. stark verschieden sind. - Hierher: V. ovata Vent., am Cap sehr verbreitet, V. reniformis R.Br. in Australien und Tasmanien überall vertreten; V. parnassiifolia R.Br., V. violifolia F.Muell., V. lasiosperma F.Muell., V. albiflora F.Muell. auf Westaustralien beschränkt. Inflorescence axis beam-like, leafless or with one to few small bracts, which are strongly different from the basal leaves. - Includes: V. ovata Vent., very common at the Cape [of Good Hope, South Africa], V. reniformis R.Br., represented throughout Australia and Tasmania; V. parnassiifolia R.Br., V. violifolia F.Muell., V. lasiosperma F.Muell., V. albiflora F.Muell., limited to Western Australia.

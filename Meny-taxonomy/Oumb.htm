Villarsia umbricola Aston
- Aston, H.I. 1969. The genus Villarsia (Menyanthaceae) in Australia. Muelleria 2: 3-63.
Herba aquatica vel semiaquatica, erecta usque ad reclinatam, gracilis, caespitosa, perennis, plerumque 30-75 cm. alta, panicula patenti multifloraque praedita. Corolla flava, 11-31 (±21.7) mm. diam.; pedicell capsulae maturae (5-) 10-40 (-62) mm. longi, leniter recurvantes; semina (0.8-) 1-1.4 (-1.6) mm. longa, pallide ochracea usque ad cinnamomeas, nunquam cinerea vel nigra. Aquatic or semi-aquatic herb, erect or recclined, slender, caespitose, perennial, most often 30-75 cm tall. Panicle open and many-flowered. Corolla yellow, 11-31 (±21.7) mm in diameter. Pedicels of mature capsules (5-)10-40(-62) mm long, gently recurving. Seeds (0.8-)1-1.4(-1.6) mm long, pale ochre to cinnamon, never ashen or black.
A V. reniformi R.Br. recedit sic: absentia stolonum; laminis foliorum minus dorsiventralibus, tenuis textis, pallidius flavo-virentibus, undulatis etiamsi natantibus (tum denique super aquam non prone jacentibus); culmis tenuioribus, debilioribus, plerumque semireclinatis; pedicellis longis in verticillis opinatis (partum usque ad 6) saepe constipatis, recurvatis in capsulis maturis; diametro parviore floris expansi. In characteribus seminis etiam differt. Differs from V. reniformis R.Br. thus: Absence of stolons. Laminae of leaves less dorsiventral, thin-textured, pale yellow-green, undulate even if floating (then finally upon the water, not inclined forward). Culms rather thin, rather weak, mostly semireclined. Pedicels long in supposed verticels (up to six parts), often crowded together, recurved in mature capsules. Diameter smaller in expanded flowers. It differs also in characters of the seed.

Limnanthemum microphyllum (A.St.-Hil.) Griseb.
- Grisebach, A. 1839. Genera et Species Gentianearum. p. 364-365.
L. Humboldtiano m. adde obs.: Cl. St. Hilaire (Voy. 2. p. 415) plures species Brasilienses distinguit, quarum tres formis L. Humboldtiani adscribo, quartam corolla lutea distinctissimam censeo nec tamen vidi. Itaque ex adumbrationibus ejus 1) Villarsiam platyphyllam St. Hil. eandem esse formam judico, atque ipsam Humboldtianum; 2) V. Humboldtianum St. Hil. nostram formam β.; quam tamen nunquam "seminibus tuberculatis" ludere vidi; 3) V. communem St. Hil. ejus lusum graciliorem. Tandem post L. Humb. haec species inseratur: Addendum to L. humboldtianum: St. Hilaire (Voy. 2. p. 415) distinguishes many Brazilian species, of which I ascribe three to the form of L. humboldtianum, the fourth I deem distinct by its yellow corolla, although I have not seen it. Therefore from his descriptions: 1) I judge Villarsia platyphylla St. Hil. to be the same form as L. humboldtianum; 2) I have seen V. humboldtiana St. Hil. to be our form β.; however the "tuberculate seeds" eluded me; 3) V. communis St. Hil., his more slender puzzle. Thus after L. humboldtianum this species is inserted:
L. microphyllum (St. Hil. sub Villarsia) "sepalis - capsulam aequantibus, corollae luteae lobis fimbriatis, basi squama margine fimbriata (glandula et corona nob.) auctis, stylo breviter bilamellato, seminibus tuberculatis." L. microphyllum (under Villarsia in St. Hil.) "Sepals equaling the capsule. Lobes of the yellow corolla fimbriate, with scale at the base (glandule and corona to us) having a fimbriate margin. Style shortly bilamellate. Seeds tuberculate."
Adumbr. "Caulis gracilis. Folia 9-12"' lata orbiculari-reniformis subrepanda, petiolis 3-4"' longo." Itaque a L. geminato et Wightiano nectario corollae, a L. Eckloniano et lacunoso fimbriis, a L. Fraseriano costarum reti deficiente, a ceteris corolla tota lutea etc. diversum. Outline. "Stem slender. Leaves 9-12 lin [1.9-2.5 cm] broad, orbicular-reniform, subrepand. Petioles 3-4 lin [6.4-8.5 mm] long." Thus it differs from L. geminatum and L. wightianum by the nectary of the corolla, from L. ecklonianum and L. lacunosum by the fringes, from L. fraserianum by lacking a net of ribs, from others by the entirely yellow corolla, etc.
Patria. Torocaba prov. S. Paullo Brasiliae (A. St. Hil.). Range. Torocaba prov. S. Paulo, Brazil (A. St. Hil.).
Villarsia microphylla A.St.-Hil.
- Saint-Hilaire, A. 1833. Voyage dans le district des Diamans et sur le litoral du Bresil. v. 2 p. 413-416.
Caule gracili; foliis parvis, orbiculari-reniformibus, [sub]repandis; corollae divisuris luteis. Stem slender. Leaves small, orbicular-reniform, sub-repand. Corolla divisions yellow.
Caulis gracilis. Fol. circiter 9-12 l. lata, orbiculari-reni[fo]rmia, subrepanda, saltem siccatione undulata, subtus [sa]epe obscure violacea: petioli 3-4 l. longi, plus minus gracil[es]. Cal. foliola obtusiuscula. Cor. omnino lutea. Squamae [tub]i suborbiculares, margine fimbriatae. Styli lamellae 3-an[g]ulares. Caps. calyce persistente longitudine subaequales. Se[mi]na globoso-lenticularia, tuberculata. Stems slender. Leaves around 9-12 lin [1.9-2.5 cm] broad, orbicular-reniform, sub-repand, undulate (at least when dry), underside often faintly violet. Petioles 3-4 lin [6.4-8.5 mm] long, more or less slender. Calyx lobes rather obtuse. Corolla wholly yellow. Tube scales suborbicular, fimbriate at the margin. Style lamellae three-angled. Capsule with a persistent calyx, sub-equal in length. Seeds globose-lenticular, tuberculate.
In rivulo quodam [re]cta haud longe ab urbe Sorocába, prov. S. Pauli. In a certain river, hardly far from the city of Sorocába, province S. Paulo.

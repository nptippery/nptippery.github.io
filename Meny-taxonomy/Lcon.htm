Villarsia congestiflora F.Muell.
- von Mueller, F. 1868. Fragmenta Phytographiae Australiae. v. 6 p. 136-142.
Annua, erecta, glabra, caule distanter foliato, foliis radicalibus nullis paucisve cum caulinis membraneis rhombeo- v. cordato-rotundis praeter basim truncatum repando- v. grosso-dentatis petiolo aequilongo v. longiore praditis, supremis in formam ovatam vergentibus, panicula cymosa confertiflora, pedicellis perbrevibus v. nullis, calycis laciniis semilanceolatis acuminatis capsulam superantibus, corollae luteae lobis ecristatis margine nudis, fauce fimbriata, antheris ex ovato sagittatis obtusis, stylo brevi, stigmatibus rotundo-deltoideis, capsulis teneris ovatis distincte valvatis oligo- v. pleiospermis, seminibus opacis lividis nigrescentibus laeviusculis rotundo-ovatis leni-compressis immarginatis strophiolam basalem fere hemisphaericam gerentibus, funiculus semine paulo brevioribus. Annual, erect, glabrous, stem distally foliate. Radical leaves absent or few, with the cauline ones membranaceous, rhomboid- or cordate-rotund, except for the base truncate repand- or broad-dentate, of equal length or longer than the petiole, the highest verging into ovate form. Panicle cymose, bearing flowers together. Pedicels very short or absent. Calyx lobes semilanceolate, acuminate, surpassing the capsule. Lobes of the yellow corolla without a median wing, bare at the margin, fimbriate at the throat. Anthers obtuse, ovate to sagittate. Style short; stigma rotund-deltoid. Capsules delicate, ovate, distinctly valvate, few- or many-seeded. Seeds opaque, leaden becoming lightly black, compressed-lenticulate, immarginate, bearing a basal or even hemispheric strophiole. Funiculus shorter than the seed by a little.
Ad fluvios Swan-River et Murchison-River. Oldf. At the Swan and Murchison Rivers. Oldfield.
Herba digitum ad pedem alta, caules saepe 2-3 e radice fibrosa producens. Folia in formis majoribus sesqui- v. biuncialia, in formis minoribus uncia breviora et fere integerrima; nervi tenues; venae tenuissimae. Flores in fasciculos fere capitatos collecti; fasciculi tri- v. pauciflori, pedunculo unciali v. breviore sustensi. Bracteolae paucilineares, ovatae v. fere cordatae, argute denticulatae v. edentulae. Pedicelli ad summum lineam longi. Calyces denique 3"' metientes. Flores bene expansi desunt. Capsula inferne accreta, superne firmior, 2"' longa v. paulo longior. Semina ½"' metentia. Herb, 1 digit to 1 ft [1.9-30 cm] tall, producing often two or three stems from the fibrous root. Leaves in the larger forms 1.5-2 in [4-5 cm], in lesser forms 1 in [2.5 cm] shorter and often most entire. Veins thin, lesser veins very thin. Flowers in fascicles, even collected into heads. Fascicles three- or few-flowered. Peduncle sustained at 1 in [2.5 cm] or shorter. Bracteoles a few lines [1 lin equals 2 mm] long, ovate or perhaps cordate, subtly denticulate or toothless. Pedicels at the summit 1 lin [2 mm] long. Calyces measuring up to 3 lin [6 mm]. Flowers well expanded are absent. Capsule larger at the base, more firm above, 2 lin [4 mm] long or a little longer. Seeds measuring 0.5 lin [1 mm].
Affinitate hinc V. capitatam, illinc V. latifoliam attingit. In affinity it approaches here V. capitata, there V. latifolia.
Duratio annua et absentia pedicellorum plantam praesingularem reddunt. Indumentum ut in multis aliis plantis hydrophilis modo densum modo pareum. Corolla lutea; lobi ecristati, margine nudi. Capsula membranea, conica, stylo longiusculo terminata, rumpens, oligosperma, haud distincte valvata. Semina lenticulari-globosa, vix ⅓"' longa, e pallide fulvo fuscata et demum nigrita, laevia, nitida, immarginata, estrophiolata. Bracteae involucrantes foliaceae interdum haud evolvuntur. The annual duration and absence of pedicels render this plant unique. The indumentum, as in many other hydrophilous plants, is sometimes dense, at other times [pareus]. Corolla yellow, lobes without median wing, bare at the margin. Capsule membranaceous, conical, terminated by a rather long style, dehiscent, few-seeded, hardly distinctly valvate. Seeds lenticular-globose, scarcely 0.3 lin [0.6 mm] long, from pale yellow to darkened to at last blackened, smooth, immarginate, without a strophiole. The leafy involucral bracts, meanwhile, hardly unfold.

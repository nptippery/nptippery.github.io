Nymphoides spinulosperma Aston
- Aston, H.I. 1997. Nymphoides spinulosperma (Menyanthaceae): a new species from south-eastern Australia. Muelleria 10:21-25.
Nymphoidi montanae Aston affinis sed seminibus tuberculis longis tenuibus acutis, 6-plo (vel plus) longioribus quam latioribus, laminis foliorum ovatis late crenatis leniter plerumque (saepe simulanti N. crenata (F.Muell.) Kuntze) plerumque supra macula parva purpurea ad locum petiolum insertum et saepe viridibus et marronio-brunneis ornatis differt. Affinity to Nymphoides montana Aston, but differs by seeds with long, thin, acute tubercules, six (or more) times longer than broad, by leaf blades broadly ovate, moderately crenate for the most part (often similar to N. crenata (F.Muell.) Kuntze), mostly with a small purple spot above at the site of petiole insertion, and often green and brown-maroon ornate.

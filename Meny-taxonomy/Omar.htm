Villarsia marchantii Ornduff
- Ornduff, R. 1990. A new species of Villarsia (Menyanthaceae) from the Porongorup Range, Western Australia. Systematic Botany 15 (2): 216-220.
Plantae habitant in solo madido sylvae umbrosae; inflorescentia diffusa; alabastra capsulaeque ovoideae peranguste; longitudo seminum 1 mm; stigmata florum longistylorum longitudine 1 mm; plantae sui steriles. The plants live in a single moist area of a shady forest. Inflorescence diffuse. Flower buds and capsules very narrowly ovoid. Length of seeds 1 mm. Stigmas of long-styled flowers with length 1 mm. Plants self-sterile.
Tall erect perennial. Basal leaves membranous, ± persistent, deep green, with petioles to 20-30 cm long, ca. 1 cm wide at base; blades 6-12 cm long, 6-12 cm wide, cordate, the lobes not overlapping, with an entire or shallowly indented margin with remote, very small, glandlike teeth. Flowering stems several, to 75 cm tall, with cauline leaves gradually reduced upward. Inflorescence a large very open panicle. Pedicels 8-25 mm long. Flowers numerous, open for 1-2 days, distylous. Calyx lobes 5-6 mm long and corolla lobes 6-10 mm long with hairs 2-5 mm long forming a conspicuous internal ring below the corolla lobes. Stigmas of long-styled flowers linear to narrowly lanceolate, over 1 mm long. Stigmas of short-styled flowers deltoid to reniform, 0.5 mm long. Capsule very narrowly ovoid, 7-9 mm long exclusive of the often persistent style. Seeds straw yellow or brownish, densely tuberculate, ca. 1 mm long.

Menyanthaceae

    The aquatic and wetland plant family Menyanthaceae consists of six genera that are distributed worldwide. The most diverse and widespread genus is Nymphoides, consisting of 40-50 plant species having floating leaves that support clusters of flowers or expanded floral axes. Two of the genera, Menyanthes and Nephrophyllidium, grow only in the temperate northern hemisphere and each contain a single species. Other rather small genera are Villarsia (3 species), found only in South Africa, and Liparophyllum (8 species) and Ornduffia (7 species), which grow in Australia (all species) and New Zealand (L. gunnii).

Morphology

    Species of the six Menyanthaceae genera have a diverse array of growth habit, inflorescence, flower, and seed morphologies, some of which are displayed on the Menyanthaceae Morphology page.

Taxonomy

    Until recently, Menyanthaceae consisted of five families, with plants in both Australia and South Africa belonging to Villarsia. However, a study by Tippery et al. (2008) determined that this genus was paraphyletic (i.e., not a natural group). A resulting taxonomic paper redefined genera according to phylogenetic relationships, resulting in the groups depicted at right. Note that the former names are listed within the tree, and the new genus assignments are given in brackets to the right.

    Like many plant families, Menyanthaceae has a long history of species discovery, and during the course of taxonomic history the names of many species have changed. I have compiled a set of web pages to summarize the species that are currently accepted and to present species descriptions from original documents. This information is available on the Menyanthaceae Taxonomy page.

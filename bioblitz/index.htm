BioBlitz 2013 has concluded. Thank you to everyone who helped make it a success!

What is BioBlitz?

The goal: Count every living thing in sight! BioBlitz is a 24-hour effort sponsored by the Rock River Coalition and its partners to document biodiversity in Zeloski Marsh, a restored marsh habitat. Professional naturalists and scientists from the area will partner with volunteer citizen scientists, students and educators to create an educational environment for nature enthusiasts of all ages.

Who can participate?

Scientists and naturalists are invited to help compile the species list. Educators are invited to arrange student visits to the site or to associated educational events. Community members are invited to explore and learn about their local natural communities! Anyone wanting to participate in the species inventory should contact Jeanne Scherer, the Wetland Monitoring Coordinator for The Rock River Coalition.

When and Where?

BioBlitz will begin at 4:00 pm on Saturday, May 18th and run until 4:00 pm on Sunday, May 19th, 2013. Zeloski Marsh is located near Highway S, just west of Lake Mills, in Jefferson County, Wisconsin. Events for the public will be held at the marsh and the Elm Point Shelter at Korth park. Scientists, naturalists and volunteer monitors will have a base camp near the Glacial Drumlin State Trail which cuts through the north half of the marsh.

Public Events

Saturday, May 18th

    4:00 pm — Invasive Plant Hike — Wisconsin has many native plant species, but there also are nuisance or even dangerous species that arrived here from elsewhere. Chrystal Schreck (DNR Invasive Species Educator) will lead a hike into the marsh and explain how to recognize invasive species.
Location: Glacial Drumlin State Trail (see directions below; map)

    7:15 pm — Frog Walk — Did you know that citizen scientists around the state assist the Wisconsin Department of Natural Resources by monitoring frogs and toads? We'll learn their calls and take an amazing after dark walk through the marsh to find out who is calling!
Location: Glacial Drumlin State Trail (see directions below; map)

Sunday, May 19th

    10:00 am — FoGHA Hike — The Friends of the Glacial Heritage Area with Jefferson / Dodge Bird Walks will lead a hike to the BioBlitz Base Camp and then into the marsh to explore.
Location: Glacial Drumlin State Trail (see directions below; map)

    Noon – 4:00 pm — Meet the BioBlitz Crew — Find out what a citizen scientist is and how you can join their ranks to make sure the water in our streams and lakes is clean. Hear from experts about bats, frogs, native and invasive plants and wonderful outdoor opportunities in the Glacial Heritage Area.
Location: Elm Point Road Shelter (see directions below; map)

    1:00 pm — Photography Walk — Bring your camera and join Eli Wedel, professional Lake Mills photographer, for a nature photo shoot.
Location: Elm Point Road Shelter (see directions below; map)

    2:30 – 4:00 pm — BioBlitz Wrap-up Event — Help celebrate the RRC's hard-working 2012–2013 volunteers, meet the scientists and learn about their work. Survey findings will be announced at the shelter as the surveys are completed.
Location: Elm Point Road Shelter (see directions below; map)

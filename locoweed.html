Population dynamics of Fassett's Locoweed

Growth and reproduction

Long-term population survival depends on every stage of the life cycle, from seedling establishment to growth and survival, back again to seed production.

Spatial distribution

Plants are found mostly along bands of shoreline that are the same distance from water. (In the image above, circles show the relative population density in different areas.) Plants can suffer drastic consequences if they are too close or too far from the lake.

Hydrology

Because plants are found on lakes with sandy soil, water level in a given year depends upon the level of groundwater in the area. We are studying to see how groundwater affects survival and reproduction.

About Dr. Tippery

Originally from Denver, Colorado, Nic has lived in Dallas, Seattle, and Connecticut. He joined the faculty of UW-Whitewater in 2011. He is broadly interested in plant function and evolution, and his research projects are split between field ecology and DNA-based laboratory work.
